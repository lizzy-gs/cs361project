What is the Pomodoro Method?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method that involves working in focused intervals, typically 25 minutes, followed by a short break of 5 minutes. After completing four cycles of work and breaks, a longer break of about 15 minutes is taken. This approach helps improve focus and productivity by breaking tasks into manageable segments, reducing burnout, and promoting regular rest. By minimizing distractions and sticking to these intervals, the technique helps maintain consistent progress on tasks.

How do I use this?

  • Step 1: Pick a specific task to work on
  • Step 2: Start the Pomodoro timer and begin working on your task
  • Step 3: After completing the Pomodoro interval, start the short break timer and stop working
  • Step 4: Repeat steps 2 and 3 four times
  • Step 5: Take a long break
  • Step 6: Repeat steps 3-5 until your task or list of tasks is completed
